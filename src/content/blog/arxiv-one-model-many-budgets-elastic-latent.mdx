## The Gap

Diffusion Transformers (DiTs) are the current backbone of high-quality image generation — think Stable Diffusion 3, FLUX, and similar systems. They work by treating image patches as tokens and running self-attention over them. The problem: transformer self-attention scales quadratically with the number of tokens, and the number of tokens is directly tied to image resolution. Want a 512px image instead of 256px? You just quadrupled your compute. There's no knob to turn.
