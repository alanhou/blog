This post summarizes Jeetu Patel's interview on Lenny's Podcast. Jeetu is the Chief Product Officer and President at Cisco, where he manages roughly 30,000 people and has led a sweeping AI-first transformation of the company.

## Why AI Is Critical for Humanity's Survival

Jeetu argues that the survival of humanity depends on a successful AI transition. Birth rates are declining globally, creating a demographic shift where there will be more elderly people than younger ones to care for them. Historically, society has always had enough younger people to support the aging population, but we may be approaching a point where that is no longer the case.

If 60% of the population needs care and there aren't enough people to provide it, the result could be enormous human suffering. In this light, the concern about AI "taking our jobs" misses the bigger picture -- AI may be arriving just in time to fill a labor gap that would otherwise be catastrophic.

## What Cisco Actually Does in the AI Era

Many people think of Cisco as a WebEx or router company, but Cisco is a critical infrastructure company for the AI era. Jeetu explains the three constraints holding AI back that Cisco directly addresses:

1. **Infrastructure constraint**: There isn't enough power, compute, and network bandwidth to meet AI's demands. GPUs need to be connected together -- first within servers, then across racks, then across clusters, and now across data centers hundreds of kilometers apart that must operate as one coherent system. Cisco provides the networking, optics, and observability to make this possible.

2. **Trust deficit**: People don't trust AI systems enough to use them. Hallucination is a feature when writing poetry but a liability when running predictable systems. Cisco provides safety and security technology to address this.

3. **Data gap**: Public internet data for training is running out. Companies will differentiate based on proprietary enterprise data and machine data -- an area where Cisco can play a massive role.

## Transforming Cisco into an AI-First Company

Kevin Weil (former CPO at OpenAI) described Jeetu's work as transforming Cisco from an "older, slower, more traditional enterprise" to a "very AI-forward company." Jeetu outlines three key moves:

### 1. Go All In -- No Hedging

Large companies experiment a lot, but when an experiment works, they don't go all in -- they keep hedging. Cisco didn't hedge on AI. They made it clear from the top down that the company is AI-first. They reassured employees that AI wouldn't take their jobs, but also made it clear that if you aren't dextrous with AI, your role won't be relevant in the long run.

### 2. Become a Platform Company

Cisco had been operating as a holding company of 251 acquisitions and thousands of products, with everyone wanting to be a GM running their own silo. Jeetu shifted the model to a platform company: "loosely coupled but tightly integrated." You don't have to buy everything at once, but when you buy two Cisco products together, they work like magic.

### 3. Embrace an Open Ecosystem

Cisco deliberately moved away from a walled garden mentality. They became comfortable partnering with competitors, reasoning that if a customer succeeds with a combined solution, that success flows back to everyone involved.

## The Capabilities Overhang

After hosting an AI summit with leaders including Jensen Huang, Marc Andreessen, Fei-Fei Li, and Kevin Scott, Jeetu identified several key takeaways:

- **The capabilities overhang is real**: There's far more AI capability available than enterprises are currently adopting. There's a "paradox of progress" -- incredible scientific breakthroughs on one side, but enterprises struggling with adoption on the other.
- **Beyond obvious use cases, it gets harder**: Coding is a strong use case (Cisco has had its first product 100% written by AI), but applying AI across every business function requires nuance and deep understanding of how each business works.
- **AI is more than a productivity tool**: Most people see AI as a productivity tool and data aggregation mechanism. Jeetu believes this is the 0.00001% tip of the iceberg. We will see original insights generated that don't exist in the human corpus of knowledge, and the physical world will be augmented with AI capabilities.

### Think of AI as a Teammate, Not a Tool

An OpenAI forward-deployed engineer told Cisco: "Stop trying to think of this as a tool. Think of this as a teammate that got added to your team, and your framing will change." This mental shift fundamentally changed how Cisco used the technology.

## The "Right to Win" Framework

Jeetu credits his former boss Aaron Levie (CEO of Box) with helping develop the concept of "permission to play" and "right to win":

- **Permission to play**: Is it logical that your company builds this, or would another company be more natural? Do you have a route to market?
- **Right to win**: Can you achieve mass-scale distribution given your existing strengths?

If you get this right, every dollar spent on building product has an outsized return. If not, product teams blame sales for not selling, and sales blames product for not building the right thing.

At Cisco, this means staying out of B2C (no distribution DNA for it) and doubling down on AI infrastructure and security where they have 40 years of credibility.

## Leadership Lessons

### Don't Delegate the Storytelling

A board member gave Jeetu critical advice: don't think of your company story as a marketing exercise -- it's the most foundational exercise of the company. With 7-8 layers between the CEO and the front line, the "telephone game" creates massive information loss.

Jeetu personally delivers the story to teams across the organization to eliminate "packet loss" in communication. The hidden benefit: it massively simplified the business, because if the leader can't explain it clearly, there's zero chance 20,000 salespeople can convey it to customers.

> "The storytelling is not a marketing exercise after you built the product. The story is why you build the product to make the story come real."

### Critique in Public, Build Trust in Private

Jeetu disagrees with the conventional management wisdom of "praise in public, criticize in private":

- **In public**: Be direct and respectful. Focus on problem-solving, not posturing. If dashboards are green but growth is 1.5%, something is broken -- address it openly.
- **In private**: Build trust. Tell people you have their back. Don't be stingy with words.

Productive conflict requires trust, and trust requires intentional investment. But once trust is established, the team can debate openly and the best ideas win.

### Infrastructure Mindset: You Don't Get the Glory, You Get the Blame

Coming from the application layer (Box, EMC), Jeetu learned a stark lesson at Cisco: in infrastructure, nobody thanks you when the network works, but when it doesn't, patients die in hospitals. This forces an ecosystem-oriented mindset -- your success is measured by your customers' success, not your own.

## Six-Part Framework for Building Great Companies

Jeetu shares a framework of six essential elements, stack-ranked in descending order of importance. You need all six to win:

1. **Timing**: The most important and the least controllable. Amazing products at the wrong time in the right market still fail. Steve Jobs shelved the iPad because the iPhone's timing was better -- the iPad succeeded later because of the iPhone's success.

2. **Market**: Go after a large enough market, a chunk at a time. A great team in a bad market gets dragged down. A mediocre team in a great market gets pulled up. "The market always wins."

3. **Team**: Not just people liking each other, but a well-rounded team where people complement each other's weaknesses. Jeetu has a partner he never takes a job without -- if they don't both get offers, neither goes.

4. **Product**: The soul of a company. "I actually think it's unethical to have a mediocre product sold in the market."

5. **Brand**: Once you lose brand trust, it's nearly impossible to recover. A mentor warned him: "Don't ever go to a company that's lost their brand mojo."

6. **Distribution**: "Just because you build it, they will not come." You need a scaled mechanism to reach people.

### Mega Trends vs. Hype Cycles

A critical leadership skill is distinguishing mega trends from hype cycles. Jeetu's heuristic: if you need a PhD to understand what something does, it's probably not a mega trend. AI is a mega trend because anyone can go to ChatGPT, ask a question, and get an answer. Web3, by contrast, was a hype cycle -- he could never make heads or tails of a concrete use case.

When there's a mega trend, don't fight it. When it's a hype cycle, don't succumb to vanity work.

## Advice for Aspiring Leaders

### Pick Hard Problems on Great Platforms

The platform you choose and the quality of problems you pick determine much of your path. Harder problems attract better people, and business is a team sport. You won't attract A-players to start a lemonade stand, but you will attract them to solve hard, meaningful problems.

Jeetu illustrates this with a story about a tour guide named Raj at the Taj Mahal who spoke 12-14 languages and was arguably smarter than any executive -- but was making $10 a day because he didn't have access to the right platform.

> "When people start confusing life thinking that everything I've earned is because of my amazing abilities, I always question that because there's a lot of luck in this thing. But when luck does present itself, be extremely prepared to capitalize on it."

### Stamina Trumps Intellect

Jeetu's favorite life motto. You can become smart through curiosity, hunger, and persistence. But hunger itself can't be taught. AI itself demonstrates this principle -- part of why it's effective is it just keeps trying.

### Don't Be Stingy with Words

Jeetu shares a deeply personal story about his mother, who passed away after 8 weeks in the hospital. During one of her last nights, she woke up and said, "I had no idea that you loved me so much." Despite everything he had done for her, she didn't fully know how he felt because he hadn't expressed it enough.

> "If even my mother who knows me inside and out didn't know how much I loved her, there's no chance that people in the business world are going to know how you feel if you're not explicit with them."

### AI Made His Current Role Possible

When Jeetu took on the role of running all product for Cisco, he says there is "zero chance" he could have done it without AI. He didn't know anything about many of the domains Cisco operates in and needed an accelerated learning course within 3 months. He worked around the clock, but without ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude, he would have been "nowhere near as effective."

## Key Takeaways

1. **AI is not optional** -- it's essential for humanity's demographic future, not just a productivity boost
2. **Go all in on mega trends** -- don't hedge, and learn the difference between mega trends and hype cycles
3. **Become a platform, not a holding company** -- loosely coupled but tightly integrated
4. **Own the story** -- don't delegate storytelling through layers; deliver it directly to eliminate packet loss
5. **Build trust first, then debate openly** -- productive conflict requires established trust
6. **Timing > Market > Team > Product > Brand > Distribution** -- all six are necessary, but timing is king
7. **Stamina trumps intellect** -- hunger and persistence beat raw intelligence every time
8. **Don't be stingy with words** -- express appreciation and care explicitly, in business and in life

## 为什么 AI 对人类生存至关重要

Jeetu 认为人类的生存取决于 AI 的成功发展。全球出生率正在下降，人口结构正在发生变化，将会出现老年人多于能照顾他们的年轻人的局面。如果60%的人口需要照顾却没有足够的人手，可能会造成巨大的人类苦难。从这个角度看，AI"抢走工作"的担忧忽略了更大的图景——AI 可能恰好在填补一个劳动力缺口的关键时刻到来。

## Cisco 在 AI 时代的角色

许多人认为 Cisco 只是做 WebEx 或路由器的公司，但 Cisco 实际上是 AI 时代的关键基础设施公司。Jeetu 解释了制约 AI 发展的三个瓶颈：

1. **基础设施约束**：全球没有足够的电力、算力和网络带宽来满足 AI 的需求。GPU 需要互连——从服务器内部到机架之间，再到相隔数百公里的数据中心之间。Cisco 提供网络、光学和可观测性技术来实现这一点。
2. **信任赤字**：人们对 AI 系统的信任不足。Cisco 提供安全技术来解决这个问题。
3. **数据缺口**：公共互联网数据即将耗尽，企业将通过私有数据和机器数据来实现差异化——这正是 Cisco 能发挥巨大作用的领域。

## 将 Cisco 转型为 AI 优先公司

Jeetu 分享了三个关键举措：

### 1. 全力以赴，不留余地

大公司经常实验，但实验成功后往往不敢全力投入。Cisco 在 AI 上没有犹豫，从上至下明确：公司是 AI 优先的。向员工保证 AI 不会取代他们，但也明确表示不掌握 AI 的人将失去竞争力。

### 2. 从控股公司转型为平台公司

Cisco 曾经是251次收购的集合体，每个人都想当总经理，运营自己的孤岛。Jeetu 将模式转变为"松耦合但紧集成"的平台公司。

### 3. 拥抱开放生态

Cisco 放弃了围墙花园思维，坦然与竞争对手合作。如果客户的组合方案成功了，这种成功会回流到所有参与者身上。

## 把 AI 当作队友，而不是工具

OpenAI 的一位工程师告诉 Cisco："不要把它当作工具，把它当作加入你团队的一个队友。"这种思维转变从根本上改变了 Cisco 使用技术的方式。

## "赢的权利"框架

- **参与许可**：你的公司做这件事是否合乎逻辑？是否有进入市场的渠道？
- **赢的权利**：基于现有优势，能否实现大规模分发？

这意味着 Cisco 不进入 B2C 领域（没有相应的分发 DNA），而是加倍投入 AI 基础设施和安全领域。

## 领导力心得

### 不要委托讲故事

一位董事会成员给了 Jeetu 关键建议：公司故事不是营销活动——它是公司最基础的工作。在7-8层级的组织中，"电话游戏"会造成巨大的信息损失。Jeetu 亲自向各团队传达故事，消除沟通中的"丢包"。

> "讲故事不是产品做完后的营销活动。故事是你为什么要做产品的原因，产品是为了让故事成真。"

### 公开批评，私下建立信任

Jeetu 不赞同传统的"公开表扬，私下批评"。他认为：
- **公开场合**：直接但尊重地讨论问题，专注于解决问题，不是装样子
- **私下场合**：建立信任，告诉人们你支持他们，不要吝啬言辞

### 基础设施思维

从应用层转到基础设施，Jeetu 学到：没人会因为网络正常运行而感谢你，但网络出问题时，医院里的患者可能会死亡。这迫使你以生态系统的成功来衡量自己的成功。

## 构建伟大公司的六要素框架

按重要性递减排列，六个要素缺一不可：

1. **时机**：最重要但最不可控。Steve Jobs 搁置 iPad 因为 iPhone 的时机更好
2. **市场**：好市场能拉升平庸的团队，坏市场能拖垮优秀的团队。"市场总是赢家"
3. **团队**：不只是彼此喜欢，更要能力互补
4. **产品**：公司的灵魂。"我认为在市场上销售平庸的产品是不道德的"
5. **品牌**：一旦失去品牌信任，几乎不可能恢复
6. **分发**："做出来了，他们不会自动来"

### 大趋势 vs 炒作周期

Jeetu 的判断标准：如果你需要博士学位才能理解它的作用，那它大概率不是大趋势。AI 是大趋势——任何人都能用 ChatGPT 提问并获得答案。Web3 是炒作周期——他始终无法理解具体的使用场景。

## 给有志领导者的建议

### 选择好平台，解决难问题

你选择的平台和解决的问题质量决定了成功的路径。更难的问题吸引更好的人才，而商业是团队运动。

Jeetu 用一个故事来说明：在泰姬陵遇到的导游 Raj 会说12-14种语言，可能比任何高管都聪明，但每天只赚10美元——因为他没有接触到合适的平台。

### 耐力胜过智力

Jeetu 最喜欢的人生格言。通过好奇心、渴望和坚持可以变得聪明，但渴望本身无法传授。

### 不要吝啬言辞

Jeetu 分享了一个关于母亲的感人故事。母亲在去世前最后的日子里对他说："我不知道你这么爱我。"尽管他为母亲做了一切，她仍然不完全知道他的感受。

> "如果连最了解我的母亲都不知道我有多爱她，那商业世界里的人如果你不明确表达，就更不可能知道你的感受了。"

### AI 让他的当前角色成为可能

Jeetu 坦言，如果没有 AI，他"绝对不可能"胜任管理 Cisco 所有产品的工作。他需要在3个月内速成学习多个陌生领域，ChatGPT、Gemini 和 Claude 让这成为可能。

## 关键要点

1. **AI 不是可选项** -- 它对人类的人口未来至关重要
2. **在大趋势上全力以赴** -- 不要犹豫，学会区分大趋势和炒作周期
3. **成为平台，而非控股公司** -- 松耦合但紧集成
4. **自己讲故事** -- 不要通过层级传递，直接传达以消除信息损失
5. **先建立信任，再公开辩论** -- 有效的冲突需要信任基础
6. **时机 > 市场 > 团队 > 产品 > 品牌 > 分发** -- 六个缺一不可，但时机为王
7. **耐力胜过智力** -- 渴望和坚持比原始智力更重要
8. **不要吝啬言辞** -- 在商业和生活中都要明确表达感激和关心
