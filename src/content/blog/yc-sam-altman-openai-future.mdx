Sam Altman, CEO of OpenAI, returns to Y Combinator to discuss the company's remarkable journey, the future of AI, and lessons for entrepreneurs. This conversation covers OpenAI's early days, the pivotal decisions that shaped the company, and what lies ahead for artificial intelligence.

## OpenAI's Early Days and the AGI Bet

### The Founding Vision
- OpenAI was founded with a bold mission: to build AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)
- "We said, 'Okay, we're going to go for AGI.' 99% of the world thought we were crazy. 1% of the world really resonated with it."
- The company started as a small nonprofit with a handful of researchers
- In 10 or 20 years, unless something goes hugely wrong, this will be the most transformative technology in human history

### Key Early Decisions
- The decision to focus on scaling neural networks when most of the field was skeptical
- Building a research lab that could attract top talent despite being a nonprofit
- The pivot from pure research to building products that could generate revenue

## ChatGPT's Origins and Evolution

### The Birth of ChatGPT
- GPT-3 was released as an API about five years ago (June 2020)
- "It was barely usable. It was quite embarrassing."
- The team iterated rapidly, learning from user feedback
- ChatGPT emerged from the realization that a conversational interface could make AI accessible to everyone

### From GPT-3 to Today
- In five years, AI has gone from "barely writing a sentence" to "PhD-level intelligence in most areas"
- Sam believes they can maintain the same rate of progress for another five years
- The key insight: making AI useful requires both capability improvements and better interfaces

## AI Agents and the Future of Interfaces

### The Agent Paradigm
- AI agents represent the next major evolution in how we interact with AI
- Instead of asking questions, users will delegate tasks to AI systems
- Agents will be able to take actions, browse the web, write code, and complete complex workflows

### What Makes Agents Different
- Persistent memory and context across sessions
- Ability to use tools and interact with external systems
- Proactive behavior rather than just reactive responses
- "You put all that together, I think you get to a pretty powerful place"

### Cloud vs. Local AI
- The future will likely be a hybrid of cloud and local AI
- Some tasks require massive compute that only cloud can provide
- Privacy-sensitive tasks may run locally on devices
- The infrastructure to serve AI at scale is a major challenge

## Energy and AI Infrastructure

### The Energy Challenge
- AI requires enormous amounts of energy
- Current data centers are pushing the limits of power infrastructure
- "How far can we go before we have to put all the GPUs in space?"

### Building for Abundance
- Sam's vision: intelligence on tap combined with energy on tap
- The transistor analogy: AI is like a fundamental scientific discovery that society will build upon
- "Some people figured out a new really important scientific discovery and society, the economy, just got to work"

### The Optimism of Technology
- The optimism of startups, San Francisco, and the technology industry
- "That is like the natural space my brain abides"
- Technology creating abundance for others is the goal
- "I'm pretty sure we're right and they're wrong" (referring to degrowth movements)

## Advice for Startups in the AI Era

### Sam's Journey from YC Founder
- Sam was in the first batch of Y Combinator in 2005
- He heard about Paul Graham's essays and the "summer founders program" from Blake Ross
- His first startup didn't work out that well, but he kept going

### Key Lessons for Founders

#### Conviction and Resilience
- "I wish someone had taught me the importance of conviction and resilience over a long period of time"
- "People don't really talk about how hard that is"
- "It's easy for a little while, but your reserves kind of wear down"

#### Trust Your Instincts
- Develop trust in your own instincts
- Increase that trust as you refine your decision-making over time
- Have courage to work on stuff that is out of fashion but is what you believe in

#### Keep Going After Failure
- "A lot of people kind of give up after one failed startup"
- "Startups don't work out all the time"
- Learning how to keep going through failure is essential

### The Entrepreneurship Analogy
- Being an entrepreneur is like having a kid
- "The good parts are much better than you can imagine"
- "The hard parts are much harder than anyone can express"
- "You have to just keep going"

## The OpenAI Story: Key People and Decisions

### Building the Team
- The key few tens of people that did the amazing work
- Attracting talent who believed in the AGI mission
- Creating a culture of ambitious research

### Pivotal Moments
- The decision to scale up compute
- The transition from nonprofit to capped-profit structure
- The launch of ChatGPT and its unexpected viral success

## Looking Ahead

### The Next Five Years
- Maintaining the same rate of progress as the last five years
- Building out infrastructure to serve AI to everyone
- "Everybody in this room will figure out how to take that technology and adapt it to what everybody needs"

### The Role of Startups
- Startups will be crucial in applying AI to specific problems
- The AI labs provide the foundation; startups build the applications
- "You all have got to go figure out what people in the world need with this new magic"

## Key Takeaways

1. **Bet on transformative technology**: OpenAI succeeded by making a bold bet on AGI when most thought it was crazy
2. **Iterate rapidly**: ChatGPT emerged from continuous iteration and learning from users
3. **Build for the long term**: Conviction and resilience over years, not months
4. **Trust your instincts**: Develop and refine your decision-making over time
5. **Keep going**: Failure is part of the journey; the key is persistence

## Notable Quotes

> "We said, 'Okay, we're going to go for AGI.' 99% of the world thought we were crazy."

> "In five years we have gone from this thing that could barely write a sentence to a thing that is PhD-level intelligence in most areas."

> "I wish someone had taught me the importance of conviction and resilience over a long period of time."

> "The good parts are much better than you can imagine. The hard parts are much harder than anyone can express."

OpenAI首席执行官Sam Altman回到Y Combinator，讨论公司的非凡历程、AI的未来以及对创业者的经验教训。这次对话涵盖了OpenAI的早期岁月、塑造公司的关键决策，以及人工智能的未来发展方向。

## OpenAI的早期岁月与AGI赌注

### 创始愿景
- OpenAI成立时有一个大胆的使命：构建AGI（通用人工智能）
- "我们说，'好吧，我们要追求AGI。'99%的人认为我们疯了。1%的人真正产生了共鸣。"
- 公司最初是一个只有少数研究人员的小型非营利组织
- 在10到20年内，除非出现重大问题，这将是人类历史上最具变革性的技术

### 关键早期决策
- 在大多数领域持怀疑态度时，决定专注于扩展神经网络
- 建立一个能够吸引顶尖人才的研究实验室，尽管是非营利组织
- 从纯研究转向构建能够产生收入的产品

## ChatGPT的起源与演变

### ChatGPT的诞生
- GPT-3大约五年前（2020年6月）作为API发布
- "它几乎无法使用。相当令人尴尬。"
- 团队快速迭代，从用户反馈中学习
- ChatGPT源于这样一个认识：对话界面可以让AI对每个人都变得可及

### 从GPT-3到今天
- 五年内，AI从"几乎无法写出一个句子"发展到"在大多数领域达到博士级智能"
- Sam相信他们可以在未来五年保持同样的进步速度
- 关键洞察：让AI有用需要能力提升和更好的界面

## AI代理与界面的未来

### 代理范式
- AI代理代表了我们与AI交互方式的下一个重大演变
- 用户将把任务委托给AI系统，而不是提问
- 代理将能够采取行动、浏览网页、编写代码并完成复杂的工作流程

### 代理的独特之处
- 跨会话的持久记忆和上下文
- 使用工具和与外部系统交互的能力
- 主动行为而非仅仅是被动响应
- "把所有这些放在一起，我认为你会达到一个相当强大的位置"

### 云端与本地AI
- 未来可能是云端和本地AI的混合
- 某些任务需要只有云端才能提供的大量计算
- 隐私敏感的任务可能在设备上本地运行
- 大规模服务AI的基础设施是一个主要挑战

## 能源与AI基础设施

### 能源挑战
- AI需要大量能源
- 当前数据中心正在推动电力基础设施的极限
- "在我们不得不把所有GPU放到太空之前，我们能走多远？"

### 为丰富而建设
- Sam的愿景：随时可用的智能加上随时可用的能源
- 晶体管类比：AI就像一个基础科学发现，社会将在此基础上建设
- "一些人发现了一个非常重要的新科学发现，社会、经济就开始运转了"

### 技术的乐观主义
- 初创公司、旧金山和科技行业的乐观主义
- "那是我大脑自然栖息的空间"
- 技术为他人创造丰富是目标
- "我很确定我们是对的，他们是错的"（指去增长运动）

## AI时代对创业者的建议

### Sam从YC创始人的历程
- Sam是2005年Y Combinator第一批的成员
- 他从Blake Ross那里听说了Paul Graham的文章和"夏季创始人计划"
- 他的第一家创业公司并不太成功，但他坚持了下来

### 对创始人的关键教训

#### 信念与韧性
- "我希望有人教过我信念和长期韧性的重要性"
- "人们并没有真正谈论这有多难"
- "一开始很容易，但你的储备会逐渐消耗"

#### 相信你的直觉
- 培养对自己直觉的信任
- 随着时间的推移，在完善决策的过程中增加这种信任
- 有勇气去做那些不流行但你相信的事情

#### 失败后继续前进
- "很多人在一次创业失败后就放弃了"
- "创业并不总是成功的"
- 学会在失败中继续前进是必不可少的

### 创业类比
- 做企业家就像养孩子
- "好的部分比你想象的要好得多"
- "困难的部分比任何人能表达的都要难得多"
- "你必须继续前进"

## OpenAI的故事：关键人物与决策

### 建立团队
- 做出惊人工作的关键几十个人
- 吸引相信AGI使命的人才
- 创造雄心勃勃的研究文化

### 关键时刻
- 扩大计算规模的决定
- 从非营利组织到有限利润结构的转变
- ChatGPT的发布及其意外的病毒式成功

## 展望未来

### 未来五年
- 保持与过去五年相同的进步速度
- 建设基础设施以向所有人提供AI服务
- "在座的每个人都会想出如何利用这项技术，并将其适应每个人的需求"

### 创业公司的角色
- 创业公司在将AI应用于特定问题方面至关重要
- AI实验室提供基础；创业公司构建应用
- "你们都必须去弄清楚世界上的人们需要什么样的新魔法"

## 关键要点

1. **押注变革性技术**：OpenAI通过在大多数人认为疯狂时大胆押注AGI而成功
2. **快速迭代**：ChatGPT源于持续迭代和从用户中学习
3. **为长期而建设**：数年而非数月的信念和韧性
4. **相信你的直觉**：随着时间的推移发展和完善你的决策
5. **继续前进**：失败是旅程的一部分；关键是坚持
