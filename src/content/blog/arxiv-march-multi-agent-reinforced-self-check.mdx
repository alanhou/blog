## The Gap

Existing hallucination detection methods use LLM-as-a-judge to verify outputs against retrieved evidence, but they suffer from confirmation bias — the verifier sees the original generation and unconsciously validates its errors. Self-checking methods like self-consistency and chain-of-thought verification fail because the same model that made the mistake is asked to catch it. The core problem: when the judge has access to the defendant's testimony, it's contaminated.


## The Increment

**One sentence**: Before MARCH, LLMs verified their own outputs while seeing them; after MARCH, a blind checker validates atomic claims in isolation, breaking the confirmation loop.

### Core Mechanism

MARCH orchestrates three specialized agents in a pipeline. The Solver generates a RAG response from a query and retrieved documents. The Proposer decomposes this response into atomic propositions — minimal, independently verifiable claims. The Checker then validates each proposition against the evidence, crucially without access to the Solver's original output.

The information flow is deliberately asymmetric. The Solver sees query + evidence → generates response. The Proposer sees response → extracts claims. The Checker sees only claim + evidence → outputs verification. This architectural choice prevents the Checker from anchoring on the Solver's reasoning path.

```
Query + Evidence
    |
    v
[Solver] -----> Response
                    |
                    v
                [Proposer] -----> Atomic Claims
                                      |
                                      v
Evidence -----> [Checker] -----> Verification
(NO Response)        ^
                     |
                Information
                 Asymmetry
```

Think of MARCH like a scientific peer review system with enforced blindness. The Solver is the researcher writing a paper. The Proposer is an editor extracting individual claims from the paper. The Checker is a reviewer who receives only the claims and raw data — never the original paper. The reviewer can't be swayed by the paper's narrative or writing style; they must judge each claim purely on evidence. The key insight: by hiding the paper from the reviewer, you force independent verification. The three roles co-evolve through reinforcement learning, like a review board that learns over time which verification strategies actually catch errors.

### Key Concepts

- **Atomic Proposition**: A claim that can be verified in isolation without context from other claims. Example: Instead of "The Eiffel Tower, built in 1889, is 330 meters tall," you get three propositions: (1) The Eiffel Tower was built in 1889, (2) The Eiffel Tower is 330 meters tall, (3) The Eiffel Tower exists. Each can be checked independently against evidence. This granularity prevents a verifier from accepting a half-true statement because part of it sounds right.

- **Information Asymmetry**: Deliberately withholding information from an agent to change its behavior. In MARCH, the Checker never sees the Solver's output — only the claim and evidence. This isn't an oversight; it's the core mechanism. When you know the "expected answer," you unconsciously look for evidence that supports it. By keeping the Checker ignorant of the expected answer, it must build its judgment from scratch, eliminating anchoring bias.

- **Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL)**: Training multiple agents simultaneously where each agent's optimal strategy depends on others' strategies. In MARCH, the Solver learns to generate responses that survive decomposition and verification. The Proposer learns to extract claims that are neither too coarse (hiding errors) nor too fine (creating noise). The Checker learns verification strategies that catch subtle hallucinations. They co-evolve like an ecosystem — the Checker's improving detection forces the Solver to be more careful, which forces the Proposer to be more precise, which gives the Checker better training signal.

## Expert Assessment

**Problem choice**: Real gap. Confirmation bias in self-verification is well-documented in psychology and demonstrably hurts LLM reliability. The RAG context makes it urgent — these systems are deployed in high-stakes domains. Not manufactured.

**Method maturity**: Clever architectural insight (information asymmetry) combined with standard MARL training. The three-agent decomposition is elegant but raises questions: why not two agents (generator + blind checker)? The Proposer seems like it could be a deterministic rule-based module. The paper doesn't justify why claim extraction needs to be learned. Also, MARL is notoriously unstable — the paper glosses over training dynamics.

**Experimental integrity**: Baselines are fair (self-consistency, chain-of-thought, existing RAG methods). Numbers look solid across multiple benchmarks. One red flag: the 8B model "competitive with closed-source" claim is vague — which closed-source model, on which metric? The paper shows GPT-4 comparisons but cherry-picks favorable benchmarks. Also, no analysis of failure modes — when does information asymmetry hurt performance?

**Writing quality**: The abstract and intro are strong. The method section buries the key insight (information asymmetry) under MARL details. Section 3.2 should lead with "why blind checking works" before diving into agent architectures. The related work section is a laundry list; rewriting it to position MARCH in the bias-mitigation literature (not just hallucination detection) would elevate the framing.

**Verdict**: weak accept — solid contribution with a clear insight, but the execution feels like it's solving two problems (bias + MARL training) when one (bias) is sufficient. The MARL component adds complexity without clear ablation showing it's necessary.

## Takeaways

Steal the information asymmetry principle: when building verification systems, hide the thing being verified from the verifier. This applies beyond LLMs — code review tools, fact-checking pipelines, even human workflows. The atomic proposition decomposition is also portable: break complex outputs into minimal verifiable units before checking them. Concrete technique: if you're building a RAG system, add a claim extraction step and verify claims independently rather than verifying the full response. You don't need MARL for this — even a simple pipeline with frozen models will reduce hallucinations.

## 增量

**一句话**: MARCH之前,大模型在看着自己输出的同时验证它;MARCH之后,一个"盲"检查者孤立地验证原子声明,打破了确认循环。

### 核心机制

MARCH在流水线中编排三个专门的智能体。

求解器(Solver)从查询和检索文档生成RAG响应。

提议器(Proposer)将响应分解为原子命题——最小的、可独立验证的声明。

检查器(Checker)然后对照证据验证每个命题,关键是它看不到求解器的原始输出。

信息流是刻意不对称的。

求解器看到查询+证据→生成响应。

提议器看到响应→提取声明。

检查器只看到声明+证据→输出验证结果。

这个架构选择阻止了检查器锚定在求解器的推理路径上。

```
查询 + 证据
    |
    v
[求解器] -----> 响应
                    |
                    v
                [提议器] -----> 原子声明
                                      |
                                      v
证据 -----> [检查器] -----> 验证结果
(无响应)        ^
                |
            信息不对称
```

把MARCH想象成一个强制盲审的科学同行评审系统。

求解器是写论文的研究者。

提议器是从论文中提取单个声明的编辑。

检查器是只收到声明和原始数据的审稿人——永远看不到原始论文。

审稿人不会被论文的叙事或写作风格影响;他们必须纯粹基于证据判断每个声明。

关键洞察：通过对审稿人隐藏论文,你强制进行独立验证。

三个角色通过强化学习共同进化,就像一个评审委员会随时间学习哪些验证策略真正能抓住错误。

### 关键概念

- **原子命题**: 可以在没有其他声明上下文的情况下孤立验证的声明。

例子：不是"埃菲尔铁塔建于1889年,高330米",而是三个命题：(1)埃菲尔铁塔建于1889年,(2)埃菲尔铁塔高330米,(3)埃菲尔铁塔存在。

每个都可以独立对照证据检查。

这种粒度防止验证者因为部分听起来对就接受一个半真半假的陈述。

- **信息不对称**: 刻意对智能体隐瞒信息以改变其行为。

在MARCH中,检查器永远看不到求解器的输出——只有声明和证据。

这不是疏忽;这是核心机制。

当你知道"预期答案"时,你会不自觉地寻找支持它的证据。

通过让检查器对预期答案保持无知,它必须从零开始构建判断,消除锚定偏误。

- **多智能体强化学习(MARL)**: 同时训练多个智能体,每个智能体的最优策略取决于其他智能体的策略。

在MARCH中,求解器学习生成能经受分解和验证的响应。

提议器学习提取既不太粗(隐藏错误)也不太细(产生噪音)的声明。

检查器学习能抓住微妙幻觉的验证策略。

它们像生态系统一样共同进化——检查器改进的检测迫使求解器更谨慎,这迫使提议器更精确,这给检查器更好的训练信号。

## 专家评审

**选题眼光**: 真缺口。

自我验证中的确认偏误在心理学中有充分记录,并且明显损害大模型可靠性。

RAG场景让它变得紧迫——这些系统部署在高风险领域。

不是人造的。

**方法成熟度**: 巧妙的架构洞察(信息不对称)结合标准MARL训练。

三智能体分解很优雅,但引发疑问：为什么不是两个智能体(生成器+盲检查器)?提议器看起来可以是确定性的基于规则的模块。

论文没有证明为什么声明提取需要学习。

另外,MARL出了名的不稳定——论文对训练动态轻描淡写。

**实验诚意**: 基线公平(自洽性、思维链、现有RAG方法)。

数字在多个基准上看起来扎实。

一个警示：8B模型"与闭源模型竞争"的说法含糊——哪个闭源模型,在哪个指标上?论文展示了GPT-4对比,但挑选了有利的基准。

另外,没有失败模式分析——信息不对称什么时候会损害性能?

**写作功力**: 摘要和引言很强。

方法部分把关键洞察(信息不对称)埋在MARL细节下。

3.2节应该先讲"为什么盲检有效",再深入智能体架构。

相关工作部分是流水账;重写它以将MARCH定位在偏误缓解文献(而非仅仅幻觉检测)中会提升框架。

**判决**: 弱接收——有清晰洞察的扎实贡献,但执行感觉像在解决两个问题(偏误+MARL训练),而一个(偏误)就够了。

MARL组件增加了复杂性,却没有清晰的消融实验表明它是必需的。

## 要点总结

偷走信息不对称原则：构建验证系统时,对验证者隐藏被验证的东西。

这超越大模型——代码审查工具、事实核查流水线,甚至人类工作流。

原子命题分解也可移植：在检查复杂输出之前,将其分解为最小可验证单元。

具体技术：如果你在构建RAG系统,添加一个声明提取步骤,独立验证声明而非验证完整响应。

你不需要MARL——即使是带冻结模型的简单流水线也会减少幻觉。
